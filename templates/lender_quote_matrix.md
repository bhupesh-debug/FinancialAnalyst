## PURPOSE & INSTRUCTIONS

This matrix compares term sheet quotes received from multiple lenders across all material economic and structural terms. The objective is to provide the deal team and borrower with a side-by-side view to facilitate lender selection and negotiation strategy.

**How to use this matrix:**
1. Enter each lender's quoted terms as received — do not normalize or adjust figures without a footnote
2. Highlight the best value in each category using the ★ marker
3. Complete the Recommendation Section after all quotes are received
4. Update the matrix in real time as terms are revised during negotiation
5. Archive all versions (v1.0, v1.1, etc.) — never overwrite; lenders often revise terms and you need an audit trail
6. The matrix does not constitute a commitment — verify all terms in executed term sheets

**Rating System:** ★ = Best in category | ✓ = Acceptable | ✗ = Below threshold

## LENDER TYPE QUICK REFERENCE

> *Use this guide to target the right lender type for each deal at the outset of marketing.*

---

### Life Insurance Companies
**Best for:** Long-term, stabilized, high-quality assets; office, industrial, retail, multifamily with WALT >5 years
**Typical terms:** 10–30yr fixed; 25–30yr amortization; 55–65% LTV; DSCR 1.25–1.35x; lowest spreads in market
**Strengths:** Lowest rates (especially for quality collateral), non-recourse, flexible prepayment negotiation
**Weaknesses:** Conservative underwriting (no hair); slow process (60–120 days); limited appetite for transitional or value-add; relationship-driven
**Sweet spot loan size:** $5M–$200M+
**Key names:** MetLife, PGIM, Prudential, TIAA, Principal, Allstate, Lincoln, New York Life, MassMutual, Great-West
**When NOT to use:** Transitional assets, sub-[X.X]x DSCR, secondary/tertiary markets, loan sizes <$5M, fast close needed

---

### Agency / GSE (Fannie Mae / Freddie Mac)
**Best for:** Multifamily ONLY — garden, mid-rise, high-rise, affordable, seniors housing (limited)
**Typical terms:** 5–30yr fixed; 30yr amortization; up to 80% LTV; DSCR 1.25x; competitive rates (close to life company); IO available
**Strengths:** Best multifamily execution in market; high leverage available; DUS/SBL programs; Green/Affordable programs with better pricing
**Weaknesses:** Multifamily only; third-party report requirements (PCNA, Phase I/II); 90–120-day close typical; defeasance/YM prepayment only
**Sweet spot loan size:** Fannie DUS $5M+; Freddie SBL $1M–$7.5M; Optigo $7.5M+
**When NOT to use:** Any asset class other than multifamily; borrower needs speed; clean prepayment required

---

### Commercial Banks (National, Regional, Community)
**Best for:** Relationship-driven deals; shorter-term needs; construction; transitional assets; smaller deals
**Typical terms:** 3–10yr; 20–30yr amortization or IO; 65–75% LTV; DSCR 1.25–1.35x; floating or fixed
**Strengths:** Speed; relationship flexibility; recourse negotiable on some deals; construction bridge-to-perm available
**Weaknesses:** Recourse common; personal financial covenants; concentration limits per bank; portfolio limits by asset class
**Sweet spot loan size:** $1M–$50M (community/regional); $50M+ (national)
**When NOT to use:** Borrower requires non-recourse; 10-year term needed; maximum leverage needed; deal has complexity requiring specialized structuring

---

### CMBS (Conduit)
**Best for:** Stabilized assets needing high leverage (70–75% LTV); non-recourse requirement; properties in smaller markets; borrowers without banking relationships
**Typical terms:** 10yr fixed; 30yr amortization; 70–75% LTV; DSCR 1.20–1.25x; 2–5yr IO available
**Strengths:** Higher proceeds than life companies; non-recourse; available for most property types and geographies; standardized process
**Weaknesses:** Defeasance prepayment (costly and inflexible); DSCR triggers and cash management restrictions; servicer (not lender) manages loan post-close; no relationship flexibility
**Sweet spot loan size:** $5M–$100M
**When NOT to use:** Borrower anticipates early payoff; asset quality doesn't support defeasance cost; heavy covenant restrictions unacceptable; complex structuring needed

---

### Debt Funds (Private Credit)
**Best for:** Bridge loans; transitional assets; lease-up; value-add; construction; loans with complexity or hair that banks/life companies won't touch
**Typical terms:** 2–5yr floating (SOFR+300–600bps); IO; 70–80% LTC; fast close (30–45 days)
**Strengths:** Speed; flexibility; higher leverage; will lend on transitional income; construction-to-bridge-to-perm
**Weaknesses:** Expensive (high rates + fees); recourse or partial recourse common; short term creates refinancing risk; exit fee often required
**Sweet spot loan size:** $5M–$200M+
**When NOT to use:** Property is stabilized and permanent financing is available; borrower is rate-sensitive; long-term hold with no exit strategy

---

### Bridge Lenders (Specialty)
**Best for:** Immediate, short-term financing needs; distressed acquisitions; properties with significant near-term lease expiration or vacancy; court-ordered sales
**Typical terms:** 1–3yr floating; IO; 65–75% LTV or LTC; SOFR+300–700bps; minimal covenants; fast close
**Strengths:** Fastest close in market (as fast as 2 weeks with appraisal waiver); will consider non-stabilized assets; minimal documentation requirements for smaller deals
**Weaknesses:** Most expensive option; short term; many lenders require clear exit; hard money lenders at lower end are opportunistic
**Sweet spot loan size:** $1M–$50M
**When NOT to use:** Property is stabilized; permanent or agency financing is available; borrower can wait 60+ days
